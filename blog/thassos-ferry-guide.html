Thassos Ferry Guide: Keramoti & Kavala (2026)

Getting There / Road Trip · 9 min read

There is no bridge to Thassos — you arrive by ferry, and there are exactly two mainland ports to choose from. Pick the right one and you roll off the boat 15–25 minutes from your bed. Pick the wrong one in August and you lose two hours in a car queue under the sun.

This guide gives you both Thassos ferry routes honestly: which port for drivers, which for flyers, real crossing times and frequencies, where to buy car tickets, and exactly how to dodge the peak-August queue. By the end you'll know which boat to aim for and how long the last hop to the west coast really takes.

Key takeaways

  • Two ferry routes only: Keramoti → Limenas (short, frequent) and Kavala → Skala Prinos (longer, west coast).
  • Driving from the Balkans? Use Keramoti — closest, most frequent, flat easy final stretch.
  • Flying into Kavala (KVA)? Use Kavala → Skala Prinos — it lands 10–15 min from the west coast.
  • Buy car tickets at the port booth, not online; keep cash ready.
  • Beat the August car queue by taking early-morning or after-19:00 sailings.

The two ferry routes at a glance

Thassos is served by two car-ferry routes from the mainland, and they leave from two different towns. Knowing the difference before you set off saves a lot of pain:

  • Keramoti → Limenas (Thassos Town): the short, frequent crossing — about 40–45 minutes. Keramoti is the small port closest to the Bulgarian and Romanian road approach. Lands on the north-east corner of the island at Limenas, the main town.
  • Kavala → Skala Prinos: the longer crossing — about 1 hour 15 minutes, with fewer sailings per day. Kavala is the bigger city (and has the airport, KVA). Lands on the west coast at Skala Prinos, which is much closer to villages like Skala Sotiros.

Both carry cars, camper vans and motorbikes. Both are run by the local ferry cooperatives (you'll see the same blue-and-white boats year after year). Neither needs booking weeks ahead for a normal car — it's turn-up-and-go — but timing matters in August, which we cover below.

Keramoti → Limenas: the route for drivers

If you are driving down from the Balkans — Romania, Bulgaria, Serbia, North Macedonia — Keramoti is almost always your port. The motorway from Sofia and Drama brings you straight to the Kavala–Keramoti area, and the last stretch into Keramoti is flat, easy and short, not mountain driving. You won't be fighting hairpins with a loaded boot at the end of a long day.

Crossing facts:

  • Duration: ~40–45 minutes, deck-to-deck.
  • Frequency: the most frequent route on the island — roughly every 30–60 minutes through the day in summer, fewer in winter. In practice you rarely wait more than an hour for the next boat.
  • Lands at: Limenas (Thassos Town), the island capital, on the north-east coast.
  • Onward to the west coast: Limenas to Skala Sotiros is about 20–25 minutes along the coast road.

Because Keramoti runs so often, it absorbs car traffic better than Kavala. That's the second reason drivers prefer it: more sailings means shorter queues. Once you're across, the coast road to your base on the west side is straightforward — see the best base to explore Thassos by car for how the island's geography shapes your day trips.

Kavala → Skala Prinos: the route for flyers

If you are flying in to Kavala International Airport (KVA, also called "Megas Alexandros"), the Kavala → Skala Prinos ferry is your friend — even though it's the longer crossing. Here's why: Skala Prinos lands you on the west coast, only about 10–15 minutes from Skala Sotiros, instead of dropping you at Limenas on the far side of the island.

Crossing facts:

  • Duration: ~1 hour 15 minutes.
  • Frequency: fewer sailings — a handful per day, so check the day's timetable and plan around it. Miss the last one and you're overnighting in Kavala.
  • Lands at: Skala Prinos, a quiet west-coast port.
  • Onward to the west coast: Skala Prinos to Skala Sotiros is roughly 10–15 minutes south along the coast road.

Kavala port is right in the city, so if you land at KVA you'll drive a rental car (or grab a transfer) into Kavala, board there, and arrive close to your accommodation with minimal extra driving on the island. For a quiet west-coast holiday this is the smoothest arrival — and it's why we steer most fly-in guests toward a quiet 2-bedroom base on the west coast.

Which port should you use? A simple rule

Don't overthink it. Match the port to how you arrive in Greece:

  • Driving down from the Balkans (RO / BG / RS / NMK) → Keramoti → Limenas. Closest to your road approach, most frequent boats, flat easy final stretch.
  • Flying into Kavala (KVA) → Kavala → Skala Prinos. Longer crossing, but lands you on the west coast minutes from Skala Sotiros — no cross-island drive.
  • Driving but staying on the west coast and not in a hurry? You can still take Keramoti → Limenas and enjoy the 20–25 min coast drive; or, if the timetable lines up, use Kavala → Skala Prinos to shave the island leg. For most drivers, Keramoti wins on frequency.

Transfer times from each port to Skala Sotiros, so you can plan arrival:

  1. Limenas → Skala Sotiros: ~20–25 min
  2. Skala Prinos → Skala Sotiros: ~10–15 min

Buying car tickets (it's easier than you fear)

You do not pre-book a normal car online for either route. You buy tickets at the port booth before you board:

  • Drive into the port marshalling area, follow the lanes, and look for the small ticket kiosk (πρακτορείο) by the quay.
  • You pay for the vehicle plus each passenger — keep cash handy, though cards are increasingly accepted.
  • Tell them the vehicle type (car / car+roof box / van) so you're charged correctly; an over-height or very long vehicle costs more.
  • Keep the ticket — deckhands check it as you drive on.

There's no allocated cabin or seat on these short crossings; you park, then go up to the deck or the cafeteria. On the 40-minute Keramoti boat people often just stay near the car or grab a coffee upstairs.

Beating the peak-August car queue

The one time the ferry tests your patience is mid-July to late August, when half the Balkans drives south at once. The queue is for cars, not people — foot passengers always get on. To skip it:

  • Travel early or late. The 07:00–10:00 and after-19:00 sailings carry far fewer cars than the late-morning and early-afternoon peak. An early boat also means you arrive, park, and settle before lunch.
  • Don't aim for the most obvious midday crossing on a Saturday changeover day — that's the worst slot.
  • Keramoti's frequency is your safety net. Even if you miss one, the next is usually within the hour. Kavala has fewer boats, so if you're flying in, note the timetable carefully and give yourself a buffer.
  • Have your ticket money ready so you're not the car holding up the booth.

One more honest tip: the queue feels worse than it is because there's no shade. Top up water and snacks before you join the line, especially with kids in the car.

You've arrived — now where to base yourself?

Rolling off the ferry is the moment the holiday actually starts — and where you point the car next decides what kind of week you'll have. The west coast (Skala Sotiros → Skala Kallirachis → Skala Maries) has the calmest afternoon water, the sunsets that drop straight into the sea, and the easiest parking on the island.

Skala Sotiros sits right in that sweet spot — 20–25 minutes from Limenas, 10–15 from Skala Prinos, with a short flat walk from the village to its own pebble-and-sand shore. It's one of the easiest villages to park a loaded car, quiet in the evenings, and central enough that Golden Beach (~35–40 min east) and Giola (~40–45 min north) are comfortable day trips.

Frequently asked questions

How long is the ferry to Thassos?

Keramoti → Limenas takes about 40–45 minutes and runs most frequently. Kavala → Skala Prinos takes about 1 hour 15 minutes with fewer daily sailings but lands you on the west coast.

Which Thassos ferry port should I use if I'm driving?

Use Keramoti → Limenas. Keramoti is closest to the Bulgarian/Romanian road approach, has the most frequent boats, and the final stretch into the port is flat and easy. Limenas is 20–25 minutes from the west-coast villages.

Which ferry is best if I fly into Kavala airport?

Take the Kavala → Skala Prinos ferry. Although it's the longer crossing, Skala Prinos lands you on the west coast just 10–15 minutes from Skala Sotiros, avoiding a cross-island drive.

Do I need to book the Thassos car ferry in advance?

No. For a normal car it's turn-up-and-go — you buy tickets for the vehicle and passengers at the port booth before boarding. In peak August, just choose an early-morning or evening sailing to avoid the car queue.

How far is Skala Sotiros from the ferry ports?

Skala Sotiros is about 20–25 minutes from Limenas (Keramoti ferry) and only 10–15 minutes from Skala Prinos (Kavala ferry), both along the west-coast road.
