7-Day Thassos Itinerary for Families

Trip Planning · 11 min read

The mistake families make on Thassos is trying to see all of it. The island rewards the opposite: a quiet base you return to each evening, and one beach or one sight a day — calm-water mornings, a lunch back at the apartment, and a sunset dinner. Nobody melts down, nobody spends the holiday in the car.

This 7-day plan is built around a calm west-coast base in Skala Sotiros, where the afternoon water is gentlest and parking is easy. Swap days around for weather, skip anything that doesn't suit your kids, and keep it loose.

Key takeaways

  • One beach or sight a day, from a quiet base — the relaxed way to do Thassos with kids.
  • Base on the calm west coast (Skala Sotiros) for gentle morning and evening swims.
  • Day trips: Golden Beach ~35–40 min, Limenas theatre ~20–25 min, Giola ~40–45 min, Marble ~45 min.
  • Marble Beach's final track is rough — don't take a low hire car; consider a 4×4 shuttle.
  • Self-cater small meals with a full kitchen; save tavernas and hill-village sunsets for dinner.

Why a quiet west-coast base makes this work

The whole plan hinges on where you sleep. From Skala Sotiros on the calm west coast you can reach every sight below on a short hop, then come back to quiet streets, free parking and a glassy evening sea. The summer meltemi wind hits the east and north harder, so your home-base mornings and evenings stay calm and swimmable — ideal with toddlers.

A two-bedroom apartment with a full kitchen and free parking is what turns this from a tiring tour into a relaxed week: self-cater breakfasts and toddler lunches, no door-parking stress, one outing a day. That's why a quiet village is the best base to explore Thassos by car, and why this itinerary assumes you're staying in Skala Sotiros.

Day 1 — Arrive, settle, swim at the home beach

However you came in — Keramoti → Limenas ferry if you drove down (~40–45 min crossing, then ~20–25 min to the village), or Kavala → Skala Prinos if you flew into Kavala (lands ~10–15 min from Skala Sotiros) — today is for arriving, not achieving.

Unpack, do a small food shop, and take the short flat walk to the Skala Sotiros shore for a first calm swim. Dinner at a seafront taverna — ask "τι έχει σήμερα;" and let them bring the day's fish. Watch the sun set straight into the sea from the waterfront. That's a full enough first day.

Day 2 — Slow local day: village beach + nearby coves

Keep it close. Morning swim at the home beach while the water's calmest, lunch back at the apartment, then in the afternoon explore the near-empty coves between Skala Sotiros and Skala Kallirachis — quiet even in August. There are no bars or shade down there, so pack water, snacks and an umbrella.

Let the kids run in the square at dusk like the local children do, then an easy dinner. For more swimming options within minutes of the village, see the best beaches near Skala Sotiros.

Day 3 — Golden Beach (the big sandy one)

Time for the island's most famous beach. Golden Beach is on the east coast, ~35–40 minutes' drive — a long arc of soft sand that shelves gently, perfect for kids who want to build and paddle rather than clamber over pebbles.

Go in the morning, grab sunbeds or stake out a shady end, and have lunch at one of the beach tavernas. Because the east coast catches more wind in the afternoon, you'll likely be happy heading back mid-afternoon to your calm home shore for a second, gentler swim and dinner in the village.

Day 4 — Limenas: ancient theatre, harbour, gelato

A culture-and-town day, ~20–25 min away in the capital, Limenas. The ancient theatre set into the hillside is a genuine wow for kids (and an easy walk up through pine), with the acropolis above it for anyone wanting the view. Wander the harbour, browse the shops you don't get in the village, and have lunch by the water.

Keep the afternoon light — an ice cream, a potter around the old town, then drive back for an early evening swim at home. Town in the day, quiet at night: the best of both.

Day 5 — Giola natural pool (go early)

One of Thassos's signature sights: Giola, a natural rock pool carved into the cliffs in the south, ~40–45 min from Skala Sotiros. There's a short walk down from the parking area, so wear proper shoes — not flip-flops — and bring water.

Go early. It fills up and the midday sun is fierce on the rock. Older kids and teens love jumping into the deep, clear pool; with younger children, it's more a look-and-paddle marvel than a swim spot — judge it on the day. Back in time for a calm home swim and dinner.

Day 6 — Choose your adventure: Marble Beach or Aliki

Pick the one that suits your car and your crew:

  • Saliara / Marble Beach (north, ~45 min): dazzling white marble pebbles and turquoise water — but the final stretch is a rough track. Comfortable in a higher car, jarring (and unwise) in a low hire car. There are 4×4 shuttle options locally if you'd rather not drive it. Stunning, with a warning.
  • Aliki / Paradise (south-east, ~50 min): twin sandy coves, an ancient marble quarry and ruins by the sea — calmer driving and lovely for kids, just busy and tight for parking in peak season, so arrive earlier.

Either way, it's one big outing — come home, swim, and have an early dinner.

Day 7 — Boat trip + a hill-village sunset dinner

Finish on the water and end up the hill. Take a boat trip — day cruises run from Limenaria and Limenas around the coast with swim stops in coves you can't reach by car. Kids love a boat day, and someone else does the driving.

For your last evening, drive 10–15 minutes up to a hill village — Sotiras, Maries or Kallirachi — for grilled meat or goat and a final sunset over the western sea. A proper send-off, and a reason to come back.

Tips to keep the week relaxed

  • One thing a day. Resist doubling up — the calm is the point.
  • Mornings for big beaches, evenings for the home shore, when the west-coast water is calmest.
  • Self-cater the small meals. A full kitchen means easy toddler breakfasts and lunches; save tavernas for dinner.
  • Buy car ferry tickets at the port booth, and in peak August take an earlier or later sailing to skip the car queue.
  • Mind the tracks. Marble Beach's approach is rough — don't take a low hire car down it.

Frequently asked questions

How many days do you need in Thassos with kids?

About 7 days is ideal — enough to do one beach or sight a day at a relaxed pace from a single base, see the highlights (Golden Beach, Limenas, Giola, a boat trip) and still have plenty of calm pool-and-swim days, without living in the car.

What's the best base for a family itinerary on Thassos?

A quiet west-coast village like Skala Sotiros: calm afternoon water, walk-to-beach mornings, easy free parking, and short drives to every major sight. An apartment with a kitchen makes self-catering with kids far easier than a hotel room.

Is Marble Beach suitable for families with a normal car?

The beach itself is stunning, but the final access track is rough and not suitable for a low hire car. Either use a higher-clearance vehicle, take a local 4×4 shuttle, or choose Aliki/Golden Beach instead for an easier family drive.

Should we do a boat trip in Thassos with children?

Yes — day cruises from Limenaria and Limenas include swim stops in coves you can't reach by car, and kids love a day on the water. It's a great way to finish the week without any driving.

How do we get to Thassos by ferry with a car?

If you're driving down, use Keramoti → Limenas (the short ~40–45 min crossing, most frequent). If you flew into Kavala, use Kavala → Skala Prinos, which lands on the west coast about 10–15 minutes from Skala Sotiros. Buy car tickets at the port booth.
