## PART 8 — LOCAL EXPERT EDGE

Specifics big travel sites miss — write these into the articles so the content reads as lived, not scraped.

**Beaches & swimming**
- Skala Sotiros' own pebble-and-sand shoreline is a flat, short walk from the village — the practical truth families want: you can do a morning swim, walk back for lunch in the apartment, and return for the calm evening sea.
- The **west coast (Skala Sotiros → Skala Kallirachis → Skala Maries) has the calmest afternoon water** because the prevailing summer meltemi hits the east/north harder. Name this — it's why toddlers and nervous swimmers prefer this side.
- For a wilder swim, the small coves between Skala Sotiros and Skala Kallirachis stay near-empty even in August — pack water and shade, there are no bars.
- Day-trip targets to name with honest drive times from Skala Sotiros: **Golden Beach ~35–40 min** (east), **Saliara/Marble Beach ~45 min** (north, 4×4-ish track — warn readers), **Giola natural pool ~40–45 min** (south, short walk down, go early), **Paradise/Aliki ~50 min** (south-east).

**Tavernas & food (west coast)**
- Eat where the cars have Greek plates: the small seafront tavernas in Skala Sotiros and neighbouring **Skala Kallirachis** serve the day's catch and house wine — slower service, real cooking. Tell readers to ask "τι έχει σήμερα;" (what's fresh today).
- Up in the old villages (**Sotiras, Maries, Kallirachi** in the hills) the tavernas do grilled meat, goat, and mountain views — a 10–15 min drive up for sunset dinner.
- Buy local: Thassos **honey, olive oil, and olives** are genuinely worth carrying home; the mountain villages and roadside producers beat the resort shops on price and authenticity.

**Sunsets (west-coast superpower)**
- This is the side of the island where the **sun sets into the sea**, not behind a mountain — the single biggest reason couples and photographers should pick the west coast. Best spots: the Skala Sotiros seafront itself, the road up to **Sotiras village**, and anywhere along the Skala Kallirachis waterfront.

**Family practicalities**
- The apartment's **full kitchen + free parking + walk-to-beach** combo is the real family unlock: self-cater breakfast and toddler meals, drive out for one beach/sight a day, no parking stress at the door.
- Beyond the beach: the **ancient theatre and acropolis in Limenas** (~20–25 min), the **Monastery of Archangel Michael** (south, dramatic clifftop, ~45 min), short **olive-grove and village walks** right around Skala Sotiros, and easy boat-trip departures from Limenaria/Limenas.

**Parking reality (road-trippers care about this)**
- Skala Sotiros is one of the **easier villages to park** — quiet streets, free parking, room for a loaded family car. Contrast honestly with Limenas and Potos in August, where parking is a genuine hassle. This is a real differentiator for people driving from the Balkans with a full boot.

**Ferries & arrival (the two ports — get this exactly right)**
- **Keramoti → Thassos (Limenas):** the short crossing (~40–45 min), most frequent, the one to use **if you're driving down** — Keramoti is closer to the Bulgaria/Romania road approach and lands you at Limenas, ~20–25 min from Skala Sotiros.
- **Kavala → Thassos (Skala Prinos):** longer crossing (~1h 15m), fewer sailings, lands at **Skala Prinos** on the *west coast* — actually **closer to Skala Sotiros** (~10–15 min) and useful if you've flown into Kavala (KVA) airport. Tell readers: arriving by air → Kavala/Prinos; arriving by car from the north → Keramoti.
- Buy car-ferry tickets at the port booth (no complex pre-booking needed in shoulder season); in peak August aim for earlier/later sailings to skip the car queue.

**Driving routes (Balkan road-trip specifics)**
- From **Bulgaria**: the natural route is down through **Sofia → border → Drama/Kavala → Keramoti**; an easy one-day drive for most BG families.
- From **Romania**: **through Bulgaria (Ruse/Giurgiu) → Sofia → Kavala → Keramoti** — typically a long single day or an overnight; flag the Bulgarian vignette + Greek tolls.
- From **Serbia / North Macedonia**: down via **Thessaloniki → Kavala → Keramoti** — Thessaloniki is the natural mid-point/overnight.
- Practical truths to include: Greek motorway **tolls** (cash/card), **vignettes** for Bulgaria, fuel cheaper in some legs, and that the last stretch to Keramoti is easy flat road — not mountain driving.

**Village life & authenticity**
- Skala Sotiros is a working seaside village, not a resort strip: morning fishing boats, the kafeneio, church bells, kids playing in the square at dusk. Sell the *quiet* and the *real Greece* — it's exactly what the resort villages have lost and what couples and culture-minded families are actually searching for.
- Off-season (May & September): warm sea, half-price calm, locals have time to chat — perfect for long-stay/nomad framing.
